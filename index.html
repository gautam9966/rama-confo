RAMA CONFRO 1.0

As we all are aware that Faculty of Engineering & Technology is going to organize an inter-debate competition “RAMA CONFRO -1.0” on 5 March, 2020 . So all faculty members & staff are here by informed that they have to actively participate and ensure the successful completion of following tasks. The following faculty members are assigned to take the following responsibility for “RAMA CONFRO -1.0” .

How To Be a Part Of The Debate
  • Create a team of 4 students with 2 alternates from your institute /College . Alternates are strongly recommended as your team’s insurance in case of sickness or unforeseen circumstances. A finalist team with less than four team members will not be able to debate. An alternate finalist team for each side will be named beforehand and should be prepared to enter the debate on short notice.
  • All team members must be registered to attend the Debate .
  • All team members must complete the application form on the website.
  • Deadline to apply to be announced.
Debate Rule
  • There should be two teams (A proposition side and the opposition side). Each consisting of four members/speakers
  • Format: A round of team policy debate consists of eight speeches. The first four speeches are called constructive speeches. The last four speeches are called rebuttals. Here is a table of the eight speeches and their time limits:
Speech: Time (MIN):
1 AC 5
2NC 5
1 NR 5
2AC 5
1NC 5
1AR 5
2NR 5
2AR 5
  • Two things are of interest in this debate format. First, the affirmative team both begins and ends the debate. Second, the negative team has two speeches in a row.
  • Resolutions in team policy debate are always of a policy nature, usually governmental policy. The affirmative team almost always defends the resolution by means of a particular example, known as a "case"; if they can show the example (case) to be true, then the general proposition is also shown to be true.
  • Five minutes will be allowed for audience questions at the end of the presentations.
  • Timecards will be shown by a judge in the audience to assist you in meeting the time markers
  • Team policy debate is focused on evidence gathering and organizational ability. Persuasiveness is not considered important -- or at least, not as important as covering ground and reading plenty of evidence.
  • The winning team will be selected by the panel of professional judges.
  • The winning team will be selected by the panel of professional judges.
  • Judgment given by judges will be the last and final judgment and everybody should respect that judgment. If participant have any kind of problem regarding the judgment participant can inform their coordinators.
  • Any kind of misbehavior and misconduct during debate by participant can lead them to cancel their participation at any stages without giving any warning.

    • All the above rules should be followed by the participant, coordinators, judges, organizers and Every person related with this activity.
